Trypsin-Chymotrypsin

CAS No.: trypsin 9002-07-7 / chymotrypsin 9004-07-3  |  EC No.: Trypsin 3.4.21.4 / chymotrypsin 3.4.21.1  |  EINECS No.: trypsin 232-650-8 / chymotrypsin 232-671-4

Synonyms: Mixed pancreatic enzymes, pancreatic protease blend, protease complex from pancreas, protease enzyme mix, proteolytic enzyme compound, trypsin-chymotrypsin complex/mixture

Product Summary:

Trypsin–Chymotrypsin is a proteolytic enzyme blend containing both trypsin and chymotrypsin, two serine proteases typically derived from porcine or bovine pancreas. The combination is used to enhance proteolysis by broadening substrate specificity, as trypsin cleaves basic amino acids (lysine, arginine) while chymotrypsin targets aromatic residues (phenylalanine, tyrosine, tryptophan).

Function and Mechanism of Action

Trypsin hydrolyzes peptide bonds after lysine and arginine residues, working via a catalytic triad: Ser195, His57, and Asp102.

Chymotrypsin cleaves after aromatic amino acids and shares similar serine protease mechanism and catalytic triad.

Combination of the two enzymes enhances proteolytic digestion across a wider range of protein substrates, useful in both therapeutic applications and biochemical assays.

  • Optimal conditions: pH 7.0-8.5, temperature 37°C.
  • Inhibitors: soybean trypsin inhibitor, TLCK, TPCK, PMSF.
  • Activator: calcium ions (for stability).

Applications in Scientific Research:

Trypsin–Chymotrypsin is a versatile, well-characterized enzyme blend used extensively in proteomic research, cell biology, and pharmaceutical applications. Its broad specificity, robust activity at physiological pH, and established safety profile make it an indispensable reagent in scientific and industrial workflows. Application highlights:

  • Proteomics and mass spectrometry (MS): Used for complete enzymatic hydrolysis in proteomics and structural studies; preparation of peptides for MS.
  • Cell culture: Employed in tissue dissociation protocols and in stem cell research and used for cell passaging in tissue culture.

Packaging & Storage:

  • Source: porcine or bovine pancreas
  • Available as lyophilized powder.
  • Store in an airtight container at a temperature of 2 to 8°C, protected from light.
