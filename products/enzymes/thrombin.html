Thrombin

CAS No.: 9002-04-4  |  EC No.: 3.4.21.5  |  EINECS No.: 232-646-4

Synonyms: Activated Factor II, coagulation Factor IIa, Factor IIa, serine protease thrombin, thrombic enzyme

Product Summary:

Thrombin is a critical serine protease responsible for catalyzing fibrin clot formation and regulating multiple steps in the coagulation cascade. With optimal performance at physiological pH and temperature, it plays indispensable roles in biomedicine, pharmaceutical development, and tissue engineering. Its safety and ease of handling—especially in lyophilized forms—make it a valuable tool in both clinical and research laboratories.

Function

Thrombin catalyzes the conversion of fibrinogen (soluble) into fibrin (insoluble), forming the structural mesh of a blood clot. Thrombin also:

  • activates Factor V, VIII, XI, XIII;
  • stimulates platelet aggregation;
  • converts protein C to activated protein C (anticoagulant feedback loop).

Mechanism of Action

Thrombin is synthesized in the liver as an inactive zymogen called prothrombin (Factor II). In the presence of Factor Xa, Factor Va, phospholipids, and calcium ions (Ca2+), prothrombin is cleaved at two specific sites to generate active thrombin (Factor IIa).

Thrombin’s catalytic activity is similar to other serine proteases and is mediated by the catalytic triad: Ser195 (performs nucleophilic attack on substrate’s peptide bond), His57(acts as a base to activate Ser195), and Asp102 (Stabilizes the positive charge on His57). Thrombin cleaves Arg–Gly peptide bonds, especially in fibrinogen, to release fibrinopeptides A and B.

  • Optimal conditions: pH 7.0-8.0, temperature 37°C.
  • Inhibitors: Heparin, antithrombin III, hirudin, dabigatran.

Applications in Scientific Research:

  1. Molecular biology and protein engineering:
    • Used for site-specific cleavage of fusion proteins in recombinant protein purification.
    • Employed in antibody and peptide production, where it cleaves fusion constructs to yield functional proteins.
  2. Biomedical researches:
    • Used in thrombin generation assays, platelet function tests.
    • Model for studying protease mechanisms and enzyme-substrate specificity.
    • Used as a target enzyme in drug screening.

Packaging & Storage:

  • Source: porcine plasma.
  • Available as lyophilized powder.
  • Store in tight container at a temperature of 2 to 8°C.
