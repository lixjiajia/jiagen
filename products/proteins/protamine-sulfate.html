Protamine Sulfate

CAS No.: 53597-25-4 / 9009-65-8  |  EINECS No.: N/A

Synonyms: Protamine sulfate salt, heparin antagonist, arginine-rich basic protein, salmine sulfate

Product Summary

Protamine sulfate is a multifunctional agent with clinical anticoagulant reversal, nucleic acid-binding, and drug delivery roles. It is widely used in medicine, molecular biology, and biopharmaceutical manufacturing, with a well-established safety and efficacy profile when handled under appropriate conditions.

Function

Protamine sulfate is a low-molecular-weight, strongly basic polypeptide derived from salmon sperm or produced recombinantly. It is primarily used to neutralize the anticoagulant effects of heparin and to form stable nucleoprotein complexes in biotechnology and molecular biology.

Mechanism of Action

  • Heparin Neutralization: Protamine’s positive charges (rich in arginine residues) bind to negatively charged heparin, forming an inactive ion pair complex, effectively reversing heparin’s anticoagulant activity.
  • DNA/RNA Binding: In molecular biology, protamine binds electrostatically to nucleic acids, condensing DNA or RNA for storage, transfection, or precipitation. It mimics nucleohistones in packaging DNA.
  • Cellular Effects: At higher concentrations, protamine can interfere with cell membrane function, potentially causing histamine release or complement activation.

Applications

  • DNA Condensation and Delivery: Used in gene therapy and transfection protocols to compact nucleic acids and facilitate cellular uptake.
  • Electrophoresis Reagent: Applied to precipitate nucleic acids or modulate migration.
  • RNA Stabilization: Forms stable complexes with RNA for therapeutic or analytical use.

Packaging & Storage

  • White lyophilized powder.
  • Store at 2–8°C for long-term stability.
