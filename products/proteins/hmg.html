Human Menopausal Gonadotropin (HMG)

CAS No.: 61489-71-2  |  EINECS No.: 232-722-1

Synonyms: hMG, menopausal gonadotrophin, menotrophin, menotropins, uHMG (urinary-derived HMG), urinary-derived gonadotrophins

Product Summary

Human menopausal gonadotropin (HMG) is a urinary-derived hormone composed of follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH) and luteinizing hormone (LH), used to stimulate ovulation and support fertility in assisted reproduction. Its dual action on ovarian follicles and the endocrine axis makes it a key agent in fertility treatment protocols. With applications ranging from in vitro fertilisation (IVF) to animal reproduction and endocrinological studies, HMG remains a critical biological tool.

Biochemical Function

HMG exhibits the combined biological activity of two essential pituitary hormones, FSH and LH.

  • FSH component: Stimulates follicular growth and estrogen production in the ovary.
  • LH component: Stimulates ovulation, corpus luteum formation, and steroidogenesis.

By combining both FSH and LH activities, HMG ensures coordinated follicular development and ovulation, making it ideal for ovulation induction protocols, especially in cases of hypogonadotropic anovulation or controlled ovarian hyperstimulation (COH) in IVF cycles.

Mechanism of Action

HMG hormones interact with specific receptors on gonadal cells. FSH acts on granulosa cells surrounding developing ovarian follicles. It binds to FSH receptors, which are G-protein coupled receptors (GPCRs), and activates the cAMP/PKA pathway. This leads to:

  • Proliferation of granulosa cells and expansion of the follicular structure.
  • Induction of aromatase enzyme (CYP19A1), converting androgens (from theca cells) into estrogens, mainly estradiol (E2).
  • Upregulation of LH receptors on mature granulosa cells, preparing the dominant follicle for ovulation.

LH binds to LH receptors on theca cells (surrounding the follicle) and mature granulosa cells (after FSH-induced LH receptor expression), leading to following responses:

  • LH upregulates cholesterol side-chain cleavage enzyme (CYP11A1) and 17α-hydroxylase/lyase (CYP17), which stimulates androgen production (mainly androstenedione and testosterone) in theca cells. These androgens serve as substrates for estrogen synthesis in granulosa cells.
  • A mid-cycle LH surge leading to upture of the dominant follicle, releasing a mature oocyte into the fallopian tube. Without adequate LH activity, even a well-developed follicle may not ovulate.
  • Under LH stimulation, the remnants of the ruptured follicle transform into the corpus luteum, which secretes progesterone. The progesterone is essential for endometrial maturation and maintenance of early pregnancy if fertilization occurs.

Applications in Scientific Research

  • Ovulation induction in women with anovulation or polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS).
  • Controlled ovarian stimulation in IVF and other assisted reproductive technology (ART) protocols.
  • Spermatogenesis induction in men with hypogonadotropic hypogonadism.
  • Animal models for superovulation (e.g. mice, rats, rabbits) in reproductive studies.
  • Mechanistic studies on FSH and LH receptor signaling pathways.
  • Comparative studies with recombinant gonadotropins (e.g. rFSH, rLH).
  • Research on endocrine disruption, fertility control, and assisted breeding technologies.
  • Cell culture experiments on granulosa or Leydig cell hormone responses.

Packaging & Storage

  • Available as almost white to slightly yellowish lyophilized powder.
  • Store in a sterile, airtight, tamper-proof container, protected from light, at 2°C to 8°C.
