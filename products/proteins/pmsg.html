Pregnant Mare Serum Gonadotropin (PMSG)

CAS No.: 9002-70-4  |  EINECS No.: 232-663-9

Synonyms: chorionic gonadotropin from pregnant mare serum, pregnant mare serum Gonadotrophin, eCG, equine chorionic gonadotropin, equine gonadotropin, PMSG, serum gonadotropin, Gonadotropinum sericum equinum

Product Summary:

Pregnant mare serum gonadotropin (PMSG) is a naturally occurring equine-derived gonadotropin with dual follicle stimulating hormone (FSH) and luteinizing hormone (LH) activity, used extensively in animal reproduction, developmental biology, endocrinology, and assisted reproductive technologies (ART) protocols. Its unique glycoprotein structure enables receptor interaction across species, making it a versatile biological reagent for inducing ovulation and studying reproductive mechanisms.

Biochemical Function:

PMSG is a glycoprotein protein hormone with biological activity similar to two key anterior pituitary hormones, FSH and LH. It has strong FSH-like activity and weak LH-like activity.

FSH functions in:

  • Stimulating multiple ovarian follicular growth and oocyte maturation.
  • Enhancing estrogen production in the ovary.

LH stimulates ovulation, corpus luteum formation, and steroidogenesis.

Mechanism of Action:

The biological effects of PMSG are mediated by binding to specific G protein-coupled receptors (GPCRs) on the surface of target cells. FSH receptors (FSHR) is found primarily on granulosa cells in the ovary and Sertoli cells in the testes. LH receptors (LHR) are found on theca cells in ovaries and Leydig cells in testes.

When PMSG binds to these receptors, it activates the adenylate cyclase pathway, increasing intracellular cyclic AMP (cAMP) levels. This triggers a cascade of protein kinase A (PKA)-dependent phosphorylation events, leading to gene expression changes that mediate cellular growth, hormone synthesis, and differentiation.

Applications in Scientific Research

PMSG is widely used in livestock and lab animals for superovulation, estrus synchronization.

  • PMSG is routinely used to induce superovulation in small mammals such as mice, rats, and rabbits, particularly in research models. By mimicking FSH (and partially LH), PMSG stimulates the simultaneous maturation of multiple ovarian follicles, enabling the recovery of oocytes from a single animal. This is critical for transgenic and knockout model production, embryo collection for research and fertility assays.
  • PMSG allows scientists to precisely manipulate ovarian hormone levels, creating a controlled hormonal environment to study folliculogenesis (initiation and development of follicles), oocyte maturation and meiotic progression, granulosa and theca cell function and hormonal feedback loops (e.g. FSH, estrogen, inhibin).
  • PMSG is used in combination with serum hormone assays, gene expression analysis, and histology to understand how endocrine signals regulate reproduction and the mechanisms of hormonal cross-talk between the hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal (HPG) axis and target organs.
  • PMSG is widely employed in cattle, sheep, goats, and pigs to induce estrus (heat) in anestrous females, promote the growth of multiple preovulatory follicles, and improve breeding efficiency and synchronization.
  • PMSG is used in breeding programs to increase the number of ovulated eggs, improving the chances of successful fertilization and pregnancy. It is often administered alongside progesterone devices (e.g., CIDR) and prostaglandin treatments to tightly control estrous cycles.
  • In donor animals, PMSG enhances the yield of transferable embryos by stimulating multiple ovulations. After artificial insemination, embryos are collected (flushed), then transferred to recipient females or cryopreserved for later use. Study is to increases genetic propagation efficiency, especially for elite or genetically modified animals.
  • In experimental IVF settings, PMSG is used to prime the ovaries by inducing synchronized follicular development prior to oocyte retrieval. After PMSG injection, an ovulatory trigger (usually hCG or LH) is given to mimic the natural LH surge and induce ovulation.
  • PMSG is essential for producing multiple mature oocytes for IVF and intracytoplasmic sperm injection (ICSI) studies. It is common in murine IVF protocols, which serve as models for human ART research, Cryopreservation techniques, embryo development studies.
  • PMSG is used to study how hormonal environment affects oocyte competence, fertilization rates, and early embryonic development. It is also applied in understanding sperm-egg interaction, zona pellucida dynamics, and cumulus cell expansion.
  • PMSG provides researchers with a reliable way to modulate gonadotropin levels in animal models without genetic modification. It is useful in short-term and long-term studies examining hormonal influence on organ development and gonadotropin-dependent signaling cascades.
  • PMSG-induced follicle stimulation allows for the investigation of gene networks regulated by FSH and LH signaling. Researchers examine the expression of teroidogenic enzymes (e.g., CYP19A1, CYP17A1), growth factors (e.g., GDF9, BMP15), and gonadotropin-responsive transcription factors (e.g., FOXL2, SF-1).
  • PMSG enables histological and molecular analysis of ovarian remodeling, including theca and granulosa cell proliferation, oorpus luteum formation, and endometrial responses to estradiol surge. In males, allows study of Leydig and Sertoli cell activation, testosterone biosynthesis, and spermatogenesis modulation.
