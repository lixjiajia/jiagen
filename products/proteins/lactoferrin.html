Lactoferrin

CAS No.: 936541-36-5  |  EINECS No.: N/A

Synonyms: Human lactoferrin (hLF) / bovine lactoferrin (bLF), lactoferrin B, lactoferrine, lactotransferrin (LTF), LF, milk transferrin, serotransferrin-like protein

Product Summary

Lactoferrin is a naturally occurring iron-binding glycoprotein found in human and bovine milk. Its biological versatility has made it a target of interest in clinical nutrition, infection control, cancer therapy, and bioengineering. It is widely used in research and commercial products, including infant formulas, dietary supplements, pharmaceuticals, and cosmeceuticals. Its excellent safety profile and broad-spectrum bioactivity underscore its continuing relevance in both scientific innovation and healthcare applications.

  • Formula (bovine): C141H224N46O29S3
  • Formula Molecular Weight: 3123.77

Function

Lactoferrin is a multifunctional glycoprotein primarily involved in iron binding and transport. Its functions include potent antimicrobial, antiviral, antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and immune-modulating.

Mechanism of Action

  1. Iron chelation: Lactoferrin binds two Fe¬≥‚Å∫ ions per molecule, depriving pathogens of iron, which is essential for their growth.
  2. Antimicrobial activity: It disrupts microbial membranes, especially in gram-negative bacteria via interaction with lipopolysaccharides.
  3. Immunomodulation: It modulates cytokine production and enhances natural killer cell and neutrophil activity.
  4. Antiviral effects: Inhibits viral entry by binding to host or viral surface components.
  5. Antioxidant function: Chelates free iron to prevent Fenton reaction and reduce oxidative stress.

Applications in Scientific Research

  • Immunology: As an immune modulator in inflammation and infection models.
  • Cancer research: Shown to inhibit proliferation and metastasis in some tumor models.
  • Microbiology: Studied for antimicrobial and antiviral activity.
  • Neonatal medicine: Investigated for protecting preterm infants from sepsis and NEC.
  • Nutraceuticals: Added to functional foods and infant formulas.
  • Drug delivery: Explored as a ligand for targeted nanoparticle delivery (especially in cancer or brain targeting).
  • Wound healing and tissue engineering: Due to its regenerative and anti-inflammatory effects.
  • COVID-19 research: Studied as a potential antiviral agent blocking viral entry.

Packaging & Storage

  • Source: bovine or human milk
  • Available as lyophilized powder.
  • Store at 2 to 8°C.
