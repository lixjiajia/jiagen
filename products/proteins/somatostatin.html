Somatostatin

CAS No.: 38916-34-6  |  EINECS No.: 254-186-5

Synonyms: growth hormone–inhibiting hormone (GHIH), growth hormone release-inhibiting hormone (GHRIH), hypothalamic factor, somatostatin-14 , somatotropin release-inhibiting factor (SRIF), somatotropin release-inhibiting hormone (SRIH), SST, SRIF-14/SRIF-28

Product Summary

Somatostatin is a broad-spectrum inhibitory peptide hormone that plays a vital role in neuroendocrine regulation, cell signaling, and hormonal homeostasis. Its ability to suppress multiple hormonal pathways makes it an indispensable tool in scientific research, especially in endocrinology, oncology, and gastroenterology. Commercially synthesized somatostatin (typically the 14-residue version) is stable under appropriate conditions and widely used in in vitro and in vivo applications to study hormone suppression, signaling cascades, and disease modulation.

  • Formula: C76H104N18O19S2
  • Formula Molecular Weight: 1637.88

Function

Somatostatin is a regulatory peptide hormone that inhibits the secretion of several other hormones, including growth hormone (GH), insulin, glucagon, gastrin, thyroid-stimulating hormone (TSH), corticotropin (ACTH), and castrointestinal peptides.

Mechanism of Action

  • Acts via G protein-coupled receptors (SSTR1–5) on target cells
  • Inhibits adenylyl cyclase, decreasing cAMP levels.
  • Reduces intracellular calcium levels, inhibiting exocytosis of hormones.
  • Plays paracrine, endocrine, and autocrine roles.
  • Exhibits anti-proliferative, anti-secretory, and immunomodulatory effects.

Applications in Scientific Research

  • Endocrinology: Studies of hormone regulation, GH and insulin dynamics.
  • Neurobiology: Understanding neurotransmitter and neuropeptide networks.
  • Cancer Research: Antiproliferative effects, especially in endocrine tumors.
  • Gastroenterology: Inhibition of GI secretions and motility.
  • Diabetes research: Studies involving islet cell function and glucose metabolism.
  • Pharmacology: Receptor binding, signaling, and analog development (e.g., octreotide).
  • Immunology: Investigation of its immunosuppressive and anti-inflammatory roles.
