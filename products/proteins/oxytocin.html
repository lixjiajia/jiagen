Oxytocin Acetate

CAS No.: 6233-83-6  |  EINECS No.: 200-048-4

Synonyms: α-hypophamine acetate, oxytocin acetate salt, oxytocin acetate salt hydrate, oxytocin acetate syntocinon, oxytoxin acetate, oxytocic hormone acetate

Product Summary

Oxytocin acetate is a synthetic, pharmaceutically active form of the natural peptide hormone oxytocin, essential for reproductive physiology and social behavior modulation. It functions by activating oxytocin receptors that mediate muscle contractions and emotional regulation. Its applications span clinical medicine, psychological research, and molecular pharmacology. Stored under low-temperature, dry conditions, oxytocin acetate remains stable and widely used across disciplines ranging from reproductive health to neuroscience.

  • Formula: C45H70N12O14S2
  • Molecular Weight: 1067.23

Function

Oxytocin acetate is a nonapeptide hormone (9 amino acids) primarily known for:

  • Uterotonic effects: Stimulates uterine contractions during labor.
  • Lactation induction: Facilitates milk ejection ("let-down" reflex) in lactating individuals.
  • Social/behavioral modulation: Plays a role in social bonding, trust, sexual behavior, and maternal care.
  • Stress-coping molecule: With anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and protective effects.

Mechanism of Action

Oxytocin acetate functions by binding to oxytocin receptors (OXTR) — G-protein coupled receptors (GPCRs) — located on the surface of uterine myocytes, mammary alveolar cells, and neuronal cells. Activation of these receptors initiates Gq protein-mediated signal transduction, leading to intracellular Ca²⁺ increase via IP₃ pathway, which results in:

  • Muscle contraction (in uterus and mammary glands).
  • Neural circuit modulation for behavior and emotion regulation.

Applications in Scientific Research

  • Neuroscience and psychology: Studies on social cognition, trust, empathy, and bonding; Investigating disorders such as autism spectrum disorder (ASD) and schizophrenia.
  • Obstetrics research: Investigating labor induction, postpartum hemorrhage prevention, and oxytocin receptor polymorphisms.
  • Endocrinology: Understanding the hypothalamic-pituitary axis, stress response, and neuroendocrine regulation.
  • Pharmacology and drug development: Used in developing oxytocin analogs, antagonists, or nasal spray formulations for psychiatric treatment.
  • Molecular biology: Used as a standard in HPLC and RIA for quantifying oxytocin in tissues; Elucidating Gq-protein-coupled OXTR activation, PLC-IP3 cascades, and calcium flux in cellular models.

Packaging & Storage
