Carbohydrates

Glucosamine Sulfate Sodium Chloride

CAS No.: N/A  |  EINECS No.: N/A

Synonyms: D-glucosamine sulfate sodium chloride salt, glucosamine sulfate NaCl, sodium salt of glucosamine sulfate, GS-NaCl

Product Summary

Glucosamine Sulfate Sodium Chloride (GS-NaCl) is a stable, crystalline salt of glucosamine sulfate, combined with sodium chloride to enhance solubility and maintain the sulfate form’s stability. It is widely used in pharmaceuticals, nutraceuticals, and scientific research, particularly for its benefits in joint health and inflammation regulation.

Formula: (C₆H₁₄NO₅)₂•SO₄•2NaCl    Molecular Weight: 573.3

Function and Mechanism of Action

  • Cartilage Formation and Repair: Glucosamine is absorbed in the gastrointestinal tract and transported into chondrocytes, where it serves as a precursor for glycosaminoglycans (GAGs), the key structural components of the extracellular matrix in connective tissues. Glucosamine helps maintain joint flexibility, mobility, and integrity, especially in osteoarthritis (OA) and degenerative joint disorders.

  • Anti-inflammatory and Anti-catabolic Properties: Glucosamine inhibits IL-1β–induced inflammation and matrix metalloproteinases (MMPs), which degrade cartilage. It may suppress NF-κB signaling, reducing production of NO, PGE₂, and pro-inflammatory cytokines.

The sulfate moiety may aid in cartilage repair by increasing sulfated GAG content. Sodium chloride enhances stability and solubility, enabling better oral bioavailability.

Applications

  • Used in in vitro and in vivo studies of cartilage biology, osteoarthritis, and inflammation.
  • Models chondroprotective mechanisms, gene expression regulation, and GAG biosynthesis.

Packaging & Storage

  • Store in tightly sealed containers in a cool and dry place.
