Carbohydrates

Heparan Sulfate

CAS No.: 9050-30-0  |  EINECS No.: 232-939-9

Synonyms: N-Acetylheparan sulfate, glycosaminoglycan heparan sulfate, heparan polysulfate, heparan sulphate, heparin sulfate, heparitin sulfate, HS

Product Summary

Heparan sulfate is a biologically active glycosaminoglycan present in all animal tissues, typically found on cell surfaces and within the extracellular matrix. With diverse and intricate sulfation patterns, HS plays essential roles in cell signaling, development, tissue regeneration, and host–pathogen interactions. Its versatility makes it a crucial molecule in both basic and applied biomedical research, especially in oncology, regenerative medicine, and antiviral drug development.

  • Formula: C12H19NO20S3
  • Molecular Weight: 593.47

Function

Heparan sulfate is a linear sulfated polysaccharide involved in:

  • Cell signaling modulation (e.g., FGF, VEGF, Wnt, BMP)
  • Growth factor storage and release
  • Regulation of angiogenesis
  • Tissue morphogenesis
  • Cell adhesion and migration
  • Viral entry and pathogen interactions (e.g., HSV, HIV, SARS-CoV-2)
  • Anticoagulant-like effects (weaker than heparin)

Mechanism of Action

  • Electrostatic interactions with positively charged domains of proteins
  • Binding specificity conferred by sulfation patterns (e.g., N-sulfation, 2-O-, 3-O-, and 6-O-sulfation)
  • Modulation of protein–protein interactions:
    • Acts as a co-receptor for growth factors (e.g., FGFs via FGFR-HS ternary complex)
    • Regulates morphogen gradients during development
    • Binds pathogens and toxins, aiding in cellular uptake or blocking interactions

Applications in Scientific Research

  • Proteoglycan biochemistry: Study of HS–protein interactions and Analysis of heparanase activity and ECM remodeling.
  • Cellular & molecular biology: Studying cell signaling pathways (e.g., FGF, VEGF, Wnt); Understanding stem cell niches and differentiation; Research on tumor microenvironment and metastasis.
  • Infectious diseases: Investigating viral attachment mechanisms (e.g., HSV, Dengue, SARS-CoV-2); Screening of HS mimetics as antiviral agents.
  • Tissue engineering & regenerative medicine: Hydrogel and scaffold design incorporating HS to modulate growth factor release; Wound healing models using HS for re-epithelialization.
  • Drug delivery & therapeutics: Targeting HS-mediated uptake pathways; Designing HS-based inhibitors or mimetics for cancer, inflammation, and fibrosis.

Packaging & Storage

  • Sources: porcine intestinal mucosa
  • White to off-white lyophilized powder
  • Store at 2 – 8°C
